What is Signal vs. Noise?

In communications theory a signal is when your radio is tuned to the right station. Noise is the static you hear between stations. We want to make sure that our Communications team is keyed in to each area—that communications needs are on our radar and we have a regular place to discuss them. So, we host a weekly meeting that's open for you to connect with us on things coming up from your department's perspective, or debrief us on ways you're communicating.

You can get on the agenda for between 5 - 60 minutes, as needed, and we'll let you know when to drop in.

Some ways you could use this time:
  • Inform Communications of projects in your dept. or area that may have communications-related deliverables
  • Get answers to communications questions
  • Get input or advising on a message you're planning or struggling with
  • Give feedback or ideas on recent communications across departments
  • Share ideas for new or improved communications
  • Get "unstuck" on a communications-related project
